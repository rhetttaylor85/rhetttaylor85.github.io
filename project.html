Defined


What is Transient Alert? This is primarily a service provided to catch, recover, and launch all visiting aircraft to an station/airfied that are not assigned there. They control a specific area of the ramp with specified parking, where they can provide services. These include arrival, processing, departure, and any maintenance visting aircraft may need, such as refueling, or coordinating waste disposal. Additionally, Transient Alert provides Crashed, Damaged, and Disabled Aircraft (CDDAR) reovery services for an air field. For example, if an aircraft taxis to the runway and then becomes disabled Transient Alert could respond and tow that aircraft off the runway, so that normal runway operations could resume.

Required Documents


All potential contracts and contract renewals have specific document requirements.
  • Services Contract Manpower Review and Validation Checklist (SCMRV) - this checklist is a review that enbles the serviing manpower office to confirm the finctions that will be perfromed are not Inherently GOvernmental (IG). This review is gided by Public Law and United States Code

  • Performance Work Statement (PWS) - This document out lines what exactly the work being performed entails and what the requirements are.

  • Mission Essential Services Memo- This is a memo from the commanding officer basically validating that the services provided in the contact are mission esstential, and that there are no miliary members availble to perform these services to the exact parameters.

  • Inherently Governmental Determination (IDG) Checklist - This checklist has a series of yes no questions to determine is the services required could only be performed by the goverment and not commercial entities.

  • Certification of Nonpersonal Services - this document is signed by the FUnctional Services Manger and verfies that the requirement does not inculde an unthorixed personal services arrangement, either int he way the work statement is written or in the manner in which the resulting contract will be managed and overseen in accordance with 10 U.S.C 129b, 5 U.S.C 3109 and 19 U.S.C 1091

  • Full Time Equivalent (FTE) Breakout - lists the amount of contractors needed, their required specialties, and references tasks in paragraghs from the PWS

  • Independent Govemernment EstimateIGE - he government's unbiased cost estimate for a contract, prepared without contractor input, used to ensure fair and reasonable pricing and to support budgeting and contract negotiations. It lists all the Contract Line Item Numbers (CLINS) of the contract and gives the price and quanties of those items. These line quanties are what services or items the contactor must provide to the goverment. They are referenced in the invoice. The assigned contracting officer representative (COR) esures all priducts or services delivered are as agreed and satfifactory, before the invoive is paid. Each successive main series of CLIN represents another year.. So year 1 would have clins 001, 0002...etc and Year 2 would have 1001, 1002..etc, Year 3 would be 2001, 2002..and so on. each successive main series of CLINs is adjusted for projected inflation, which is usually around 5%.

The Contracting Phase


Once all documents have been prepared they are submited in the appropriate system for contracting review. This systems assigned a requistion number. Contracting then reviews the documents and ensure they are filled out satifactorily. They will then also verify funds availability. Next they will draft up the reqirements and advertise the contract out for competitive bidding. Contractors can also ask questions during this phase for clarification on requirements, Once a bid is accepted then a contract is fully drafted and sent for signature. Once signed its ALL DONE!
